The Requisite

I. The Man Who Gave Everything

I was once the man who mistook sacrifice for identity. I loved her or at least that is what I called it. I would run, crawl, kneel just to earn a smile. I spoke in grand gestures: “I’d bring you the world.” And I meant it. I poured until I was empty. Confused depletion for devotion. Translated silence into strength. Told myself this is what men do we endure, we provide, we do not ask. No reassurance. No appreciation. Just a quiet erosion I named masculinity. Pitiful, isn’t it? A man can give his life without ever living it. We are applauded as boys, but as men we are audited. What can you carry? What can you build? What can you provide? And if we break if the weight fractures bone and spirit we are told to stand taller. Some do not. Some decide it is easier to leave the world than keep proving their worth to it. Yet no one asks when was the last time a man was seen without performance.

II. The Love We Forgot

Love was never meant to be a transaction. Not flowers for validation. Not gifts for applause. Not public display to quiet private emptiness. Love was covenant a choosing, a daily inclusion, a shared obedience to something higher than ego. If you are with me, be with me. Not half-present. Not entertained elsewhere. Not treating loyalty like an optional clause. Respect the boundaries we build. Honor what we said we would become. Include me in your world as I include you in mine. And to the man who demands submission ask yourself: have you become safe enough to follow? Vision attracts trust. Stability invites surrender. Leadership is not dominance; it is responsibility embodied. And to the woman you are not a body, not a utility, not an accessory to ambition. You are a helpmate, a partner in purpose, a place of peace not a battleground for ego. Love requires two crucifixions: the death of pride and the burial of selfishness. Anything less is convenience wearing perfume.

III. Loving Me

But this this is where it changes. Before loving you, I must love me. Not the inflated ego. Not the wounded child seeking applause. But the man who can stand alone without resentment. I will not die emotionally just to keep someone comfortable. I will not abandon myself to feel chosen. Because if I never loved me, I would not recognize love even if it held me gently. I would sabotage it. Distrust it. Drown it with need. Valentine’s is not roses. It is alignment. It is discipline of the heart. It is choosing yourself so you can choose another without losing either. This is part one. Not of loving her. Not of saving the world. But of reclaiming the man I almost buried trying to prove I was worth loving.
